Building the Future.

A deep dive into Semiconductor Manufacturing & Engineering.

STEP 01

Wafer Preparation

It starts with a silicon ingot, grown to 99.9999% purity. We slice these into ultra-thin wafers, creating the canvas for modern computing.

STEP 02

Photolithography

Using DUV (Deep Ultraviolet) light, we project intricate circuit patterns onto a light-sensitive coating called photoresist. This is where the "logic" is printed.

STEP 03

Etching & Deposition

Chemical gases carve away the unexposed areas, leaving behind the three-dimensional structures of the transistors. We then deposit copper layers to create the "wiring."

STEP 04

Ion Implantation

To create transistors, we "dope" the silicon by firing high-energy ions into the surface. This alters the conductivity of specific regions, creating the P-type and N-type junctions that allow electricity to flow or stop on command.

STEP 05

Metrology & Quality Control

Precision is everything. We use Scanning Electron Microscopes (SEM) to inspect circuit patterns at the nanometer scale and Raman Spectroscopy to analyze material stress and lattice quality, ensuring zero defects before the wafer is diced.

STEP 06

Assembly & Packaging

The finished wafer is sliced into individual dies. Each die is connected to pins and sealed in a protective ceramic or plastic case to become the processor that powers your devices. The final step is the functional test, validating performance across voltage and temperature corners.
